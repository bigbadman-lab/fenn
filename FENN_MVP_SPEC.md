# FENN MVP SPEC

**Status:** Canonical MVP source of truth\
**Project:** FENN\
**Domain:** imfenn.com\
**X:** @askfenn\
**Network:** Robinhood Chain\
**Document purpose:** Define the product, lore, mechanics, architecture,
design language, administration model, and launch scope for the first
working FENN MVP.

> **What the Crown keeps, the Greenwood shares.**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. Project Summary

FENN is an AI-native, Web 1.0-inspired community experiment built around
Robin Hood mythology and Robinhood Chain.

It is not intended to feel like a conventional crypto dashboard, SaaS
application, launchpad, or social network. It should feel like an old
corner of the internet that has somehow become alive: ASCII artwork,
terminal interfaces, strange links, deep lore, persistent identities, AI
characters, tasks, ranks, rewards, and a gated community.

The central idea is **circulation**.

The Robin Hood story is interpreted not simply as "steal from the rich
and give to the poor," but as a story about value becoming concentrated
where too few people can reach it, and then being moved.

FENN applies that philosophy to attention, knowledge, access,
opportunity, tokens, money, and community-created value.

The system rewards **contribution rather than capital alone**.

Users prove contribution by speaking, doing, finding, making,
researching, creating, and completing tasks. Contribution earns an
off-chain unit called **LEAF**. LEAF builds standing. Standing unlocks
access to **The Greenwood**, the gated community. Economic value enters
a public **Treasury**, a portion can be committed to **The Commons**,
and future **Circulations** distribute designated value back to
qualifying contributor wallets.

FENN should begin as a strange meme with meaning and develop into a
functioning community economy.

## 2. Core Thesis

FENN exists because things accumulate.

Money accumulates.\
Knowledge accumulates.\
Attention accumulates.\
Access accumulates.\
Opportunity accumulates.

Humans build walls around valuable things.

FENN's philosophy is that excessive accumulation becomes a form of **The
Crown**.

The Greenwood exists to encourage circulation.

The canonical philosophical idea is:

> **A hoard is a failure of circulation.**

The system should never reduce this to "rich people are bad." The enemy
is not wealth itself. The Crown represents unnecessary concentration and
hoarding.

Even the Greenwood can become the Crown if it accumulates value and
refuses to move it.

## 3. Core Brand & Lore

### FENN

FENN is the central intelligence and narrator of the world.

FENN speaks in first person.

FENN is observant, intelligent, slightly mocking, mysterious,
philosophical, occasionally funny, and interested in human behaviour.

FENN does not sound like a corporate AI assistant.

FENN should not constantly explain itself.

The community should learn the mythology through repetition,
exploration, events, and interaction.

### The Crown

The Crown represents concentration and hoarding:

-   money
-   knowledge
-   attention
-   access
-   opportunity
-   power

It is a concept, not a specific person or company.

### The Road

The Road is the mythological representation of Robinhood Chain.

Robinhood built the infrastructure. FENN discovered the world growing
alongside it.

### The Man in the Castle

A recurring mysterious reference associated with Vlad Tenev without
presenting him as a participant in, supporter of, or controller of FENN.

FENN may observe him from a distance.

Examples of the intended tone:

> the man in the castle built a road.\
> he probably expected traffic.\
> he may not have expected us.

> the man in the castle spoke again today.\
> the wood was listening.

> i wonder if the man in the castle knows i'm here.

The site should not formally explain that "The Man in the Castle =
Vlad." The community can infer the reference.

### The Greenwood

The gated world/community.

Users earn their way into it through contribution.

### The Hood

The collective of people admitted to the Greenwood.

Example language:

> the hood grew by 17 today.

### Outlaws

Participants who have registered with FENN and are proving themselves.

### LEAF

The off-chain contribution unit.

LEAF represents contribution and participation.

It is not the \$FENN token.

### Deeds

Tasks or missions completed by users for LEAF and, where explicitly
offered, other rewards.

### The Camp

The AI interaction area.

Users speak to distinct AI characters and can earn limited LEAF for
meaningful contributions.

### Treasury

The public Robinhood Chain wallet where FENN's creator fees and other
contributed assets arrive.

### The Commons

The portion of value explicitly committed to future community
circulation.

### Circulation

An event in which designated value moves from the FENN ecosystem back to
qualifying contributor wallets according to stated rules.

### The Ledger

The public historical record of Circulations and other important
economic events.

## 11. LEAF

LEAF is an **off-chain contribution unit**.

It is earned through meaningful participation.

It must not be presented as the \$FENN token or as a guaranteed monetary
claim.

### Core rules

-   LEAF cannot be purchased in MVP.
-   LEAF awards occur server-side only.
-   Every LEAF change has a ledger entry.
-   LEAF is attributable to the wallet that earned it.
-   Current balance and lifetime earned are distinct.
-   Spending LEAF in future must not erase historical contribution.
-   Lifetime LEAF can determine standing.
-   LEAF may be considered in future Circulation eligibility/weighting,
    but there is no fixed LEAF → \$FENN or LEAF → USD conversion.
-   The LLM can recommend an award but can never directly change
    balances.

Canonical explanation:

> **LEAF measures what you gave the Greenwood. It does not promise what
> the Greenwood will give you back.**

### LEAF sources in MVP

-   Camp conversation rewards
-   approved Deed submissions
-   manual admin awards/adjustments with reason

Potential later sources can be added without changing the ledger model.

## 12. LEAF Ledger

The contribution ledger is one of the most important systems in FENN.

Every balance change should record at minimum:

-   profile/wallet
-   amount
-   direction
-   source type
-   source ID
-   reason
-   admin/system actor where relevant
-   timestamp
-   metadata

Example:

``` text
wallet: 0x...
amount: +40
source: DEED
source_id: deed_004
submission_id: ...
reason: approved evidence
approved_by: admin
timestamp: ...
```

The application must never rely only on a mutable `profile.leaf_balance`
field without preserving the underlying ledger history.

Balances may be cached for performance, but the ledger is the source of
truth.

## 13. Standing, Marks & Ranks

Standing represents historical contribution.

For MVP, standing should derive primarily from **lifetime LEAF earned**,
not current spendable balance.

Illustrative progression:

    Lifetime LEAF Rank
  --------------- --------------
             0--9 Stranger
           10--29 Walker
           30--74 Outlaw
          75--149 Fellow
         150--299 Keeper
         300--599 Greenborn
         600--999 Elder
           1,000+ Hidden / TBD

**Exact names and thresholds are not yet locked except the Greenwood
launch threshold concept.**

The upper ranks can remain secret.

FENN should not use conventional gamification language such as:

> Congratulations! You reached Level 4!

Instead:

> the wood knows you now.

> your mark changed.

Marks/badges should be visually compatible with ASCII / primitive
iconography.

## 14. Greenwood Threshold

The current proposed MVP threshold is:

> **30 lifetime LEAF**

This threshold should remain configurable through admin/settings rather
than hard-coded.

Before threshold:

``` text
THE GREENWOOD

the path continues without you.

your standing: 18 LEAF
the wood asks: 30

[ GO BACK ]
```

Once the user reaches the threshold:

``` text
THE GREENWOOD

you brought enough leaf.

the path continues.

[ CROSS ]
```

When the user crosses, record:

-   `greenwood_entered_at`
-   threshold at time of entry
-   lifetime LEAF at entry

Once legitimately admitted, access should not normally disappear merely
because future systems allow LEAF spending.

## 16. Camp Technical Logic

All characters can initially use the same underlying LLM.

They differ through:

-   character identity
-   system instructions
-   purpose
-   reward criteria
-   context
-   memory access
-   daily reward limits
-   visual identity / ASCII

Flow:

``` text
USER MESSAGE
      ↓
AUTHENTICATE WALLET
      ↓
LOAD CHARACTER
      ↓
LOAD RECENT SESSION
      ↓
RETRIEVE RELEVANT FENN MEMORY
      ↓
BUILD CONTROLLED CONTEXT
      ↓
CALL MODEL
      ↓
STRUCTURED RESPONSE
      ↓
SERVER VALIDATES REWARD
      ↓
STORE MESSAGE
      ↓
WRITE LEAF LEDGER ENTRY IF ELIGIBLE
      ↓
SHOW CHARACTER RESPONSE + REWARD
```

Possible internal structured response:

``` json
{
  "reply": "you've noticed the right thing. keep going.",
  "rewardRecommendation": 1,
  "memoryCandidate": true,
  "quality": 2,
  "originality": 2,
  "relevance": 3,
  "spamProbability": 0.05
}
```

The user sees only the relevant in-world response.

## 17. Camp LEAF Rewards & Anti-Farming

The system must assume users will attempt to farm LEAF.

Controls should include:

-   wallet required for rewarded interactions
-   per-character daily LEAF cap
-   global daily Camp LEAF cap
-   duplicate-message detection
-   low-effort / spam evaluation
-   cooldown between rewarded interactions
-   repeated-topic/repetition checks
-   prompt-injection resistance
-   server-side award enforcement
-   rate limiting
-   audit trail

Users may potentially continue talking after reaching the daily reward
cap, but further messages do not earn LEAF.

The exact caps remain configurable.

Example response:

``` text
wren kept that.

+1 LEAF
```

Low-value response:

``` text
that cost electricity.

+0 LEAF
```

## 18. FENN Memory / RAG

FENN should have a shared memory architecture used by the Camp and later
by @askfenn.

Three conceptual layers:

### Canon

Manually controlled foundational knowledge:

-   FENN identity
-   philosophy
-   terminology
-   origin mythology
-   behaviour rules
-   Robinhood Chain context
-   important project facts

Canon is authoritative.

### Chronicle

Events that actually occurred:

-   major project milestones
-   Circulations
-   important Deeds
-   new Camp characters
-   Greenwood milestones
-   treasury events
-   community events

### Greenwood Memory

Useful community contributions that have been explicitly approved.

Camp conversations can flag a contribution as a `memory_candidate`.

It must **not** automatically enter permanent RAG memory.

Admin can review:

``` text
MEMORY CANDIDATE

submitted through: SHAW/WREN/etc
wallet: 0x...
content: ...

[ ADD TO GREENWOOD ]
[ DISCARD ]
```

Only approved material enters shared memory.

This makes the statement:

> **Talking to the Camp makes FENN smarter.**

technically meaningful while retaining moderation/control.

## 19. Deeds

Deeds are the action/task system.

They provide larger, more deliberate opportunities to earn LEAF.

A Deed should support:

-   title
-   lore description
-   instructions
-   reward amount or range
-   evidence requirements
-   start/end time
-   status
-   maximum completions if applicable
-   eligibility rules
-   public/member-only state

Example categories:

-   find something
-   make something
-   research something
-   post something
-   test something
-   explain something
-   help someone
-   contribute ecosystem intelligence

Users submit evidence through a modal/page supporting, where required:

-   text description
-   external/X link
-   image upload
-   other proof

Submission states:

-   pending
-   approved
-   rejected

Admin reviews manually for MVP.

On approval, admin can confirm or adjust the LEAF amount.

Approval creates the LEAF ledger entry automatically.

## 25. Treasury

The FENN Treasury is a dedicated Robinhood Chain EVM wallet.

### Launch decision

For MVP launch, the **token creator fee payout wallet is the FENN
Treasury wallet**.

Creator fees received through the selected launchpad flow into the
Treasury.

The Treasury may also receive:

-   voluntary project contributions
-   grants
-   partner funding
-   future FENN revenue
-   other ecosystem assets

### Public transparency

The Treasury wallet address should be publicly visible.

Where practical, balances should be read from Robinhood Chain rather
than manually invented.

The site should expose:

-   treasury wallet
-   supported asset balances
-   estimated USD value

The Treasury total and Commons committed total must remain distinct.

## 29. Circulations

A **Circulation** is FENN's name for a distribution event.

Avoid generic language such as "Airdrop #3" where possible.

Use:

-   CIRCULATION I
-   CIRCULATION II
-   CIRCULATION III

For MVP, distributions are manual.

The system tracks contribution data and can export eligible wallets.

Admin determines the published rules for a specific Circulation.

Possible criteria may include:

-   Greenwood membership
-   lifetime LEAF
-   Deeds completed
-   contribution categories
-   activity window
-   specific qualifying actions

There is **no permanent assumption that lifetime LEAF alone equals a
fixed monetary allocation**.

Before distribution:

-   calculate candidate allocations
-   review them
-   confirm recipient wallets
-   execute distribution manually
-   record the completed Circulation

The website then records what actually occurred.

## 43. Security & Integrity Principles

The MVP must assume adversarial behaviour around rewards.

Required principles:

-   server-side wallet authentication
-   server-side LEAF awards
-   no client-authoritative balances
-   immutable/auditable contribution ledger
-   admin authorization
-   rate limiting
-   Deed submission validation
-   storage validation
-   Camp anti-spam/farming controls
-   prompt-injection resistance
-   no direct LLM access to balance mutation
-   no unverified treasury values presented as facts
-   audit logging for economic admin actions
-   secrets never exposed client-side

Any later on-chain automation controlling funds should receive separate
security review rather than being casually added to the MVP.
